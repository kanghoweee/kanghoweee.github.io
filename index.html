NTU Autonomous Underwater Vehicle Team - Electrical Lead

As the Electrical Lead from January 2023 to September 2023, I architected signal circuitry, troubleshooted microcontroller issues, and optimized PID feedback control. I also integrated depth sensors into the existing software stack, enhancing the vehicle's autonomy and functionality. I also conducted live pool tests to ensure the reliability of the systems underwater.

NTU Radio Frequency Laboratory - PCB Design Intern

During my internship at NTU's Radio Frequency Laboratory, I designed a high-speed wireless transmitter PCB for NTU's EEE department. This included creating the schematic, board layout, and overseeing fabrication. I focused on maintaining signal integrity and noise tolerances through techniques like ground planes and differential pair routing, ensuring the PCB met rigorous performance standards.

Google Tech4Tomorrow Hackathon - Crowd Control Algorithm

As the backend developer for my hackathon team, I utilized Python and Matplotlib to fuse computer vision data from OpenCV and simulated GPS positions. In doing so, I successfully implemented a fluid mechanics-based algorithm and adhered to object-oriented programming principles in creating the final algorithm. We presented our project at the Google Asia-Pacific HQ in Singapore, and came in 2nd place at the hackathon.
